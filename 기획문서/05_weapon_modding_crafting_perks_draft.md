# RDR2 Epilogue RPG Overhaul Weapon Modding, Crafting, And Expanded Perks Draft

## Core Goal
Add a Fallout-like sense of ownership over weapons and builds without breaking Red Dead's western identity.

The system should feel like:
- maintaining your guns
- salvaging parts
- tuning weapons for a purpose
- crafting upgrades through benches and specialists
- building John into a distinct combat style through perks

It should not feel like:
- sci-fi weapon assembly
- absurd attachment spam
- modern tacticool customization

## Design Rule
This system is inspired by Fallout-style gun care and customization, but translated into frontier language.

That means:
- fewer slots
- stronger weapon identity
- harsher material cost
- more grounded naming
- bigger differences between builds

## Weapon Management Layer

## 1. Condition
All key weapons have an expanded condition layer.

Condition affects:
- reload smoothness
- jam chance or failure chance on worn rare weapons
- draw responsiveness
- precision stability
- sale value

Condition states:
- Pristine
- Clean
- Field-Worn
- Dirty
- Damaged

This creates a reason to maintain a favorite gun instead of swapping endlessly.

## 2. Familiarity
Weapons gain familiarity as John uses them.

Familiarity affects:
- handling
- swap speed
- minor recoil control
- special perk interactions

This rewards long-term weapon commitment.

## 3. Marked Weapons
Some guns become named or marked over time.
These are not random magic drops.
They are weapons John rebuilds, wins, inherits, or takes from named elites.

Examples:
- Silas Redd's Repeater
- Blackwater Peacebreaker
- Ash-Marked Schofield

## Weapon Categories

The system supports:
- revolvers
- pistols
- repeaters
- rifles
- shotguns
- bows
- special weapons and relic variants

Each category should have different available upgrade logic.

## Modular Upgrade Philosophy

Instead of infinite mod spam, each weapon has a limited set of meaningful mod slots.

### Revolvers / Pistols
- Barrel
- Frame
- Grip
- Sight
- Action Tune
- Ammo Preference

### Repeaters / Rifles
- Barrel
- Receiver
- Sight
- Stock Tune
- Lever / Bolt Tune
- Ammo Preference

### Shotguns
- Barrel
- Receiver
- Stock
- Choke Tune
- Shell Preference

### Bows
- Limb Reinforcement
- String Grade
- Grip Wrap
- Arrowhead Compatibility

## Example Upgrade Effects

### Barrel Upgrades
- Improved range
- better velocity
- tighter spread
- slight weight penalty

### Receiver / Frame Upgrades
- improved durability
- better cycling
- stronger damage profile
- higher craft cost

### Grip / Stock Upgrades
- improved stability
- faster follow-up shots
- lower sway

### Sight Upgrades
- better precision
- faster target acquisition
- improved weak-point play

### Action Tune
- faster reload
- smoother cycling
- faster draw or rechamber speed

### Ammo Preference
This is not a universal ammo button.
It is a tuning path that favors how the weapon behaves with certain ammo types.

Examples:
- hunting tuned
- armor-breaking tuned
- high-velocity tuned
- stopping-power tuned

## Crafted Weapon Archetypes

Every weapon should gradually become one of several roles.

### Duel Build
Fast draw, fast aim, crit-friendly

### Hunter Build
Cleaner shots, less pelt damage, better precision

### Outlaw Build
High stopping power, intimidation, close-range dominance

### Ranger Build
Long-range consistency, tracking synergy, wilderness bonuses

### Relic Build
Late-game unique path with special visuals or unusual properties

## Crafting Flow

## Where Crafting Happens

### Beecher's Hope Workbench
Main home customization station.
Best for:
- weapon part installation
- field repair
- accessory fitting
- ammo preparation
- weapon naming and favorite loadouts

### Gunsmith
Best for:
- professional barrel and receiver work
- precision upgrades
- expensive tuning
- blueprint-gated weapon modifications

### Trapper
Best for:
- leather wraps
- bone and fang charms
- hunting-focused tuning

### Fence
Best for:
- illegal components
- relic bindings
- black-market conversions
- boss part processing

## Blueprint Logic
The best upgrades should require more than just raw materials.

They also require:
- gunsmith diagrams
- stolen military manuals
- boss recipes
- trapper patterns
- fence schematics

This lets the player farm both parts and knowledge.

## Ammo Crafting Expansion

Ammo should become a progression system, not just shop stock.

Possible crafted ammo paths:
- improved hunting rounds
- stagger shells
- black powder heavy rounds
- precision hand-loads
- relic-marked late-game ammunition

These should stay limited and expensive so they feel important.

## Cleaning / Repair Loop

To keep the system immersive:
- basic cleaning can be done in the field
- stronger repair requires Beecher's Hope or a gunsmith
- legendary or relic weapons require specialist restoration

This creates a homecoming rhythm:
fight, loot, repair, tune, go back out.

## Expanded Perk Support

The earlier perk trees stay intact, but this adds more weapon-specific branches inside them.

## Gunslinger Additions
- Sure Hand: reduced sway after a quick draw
- Brass Memory: faster reload on familiar sidearms
- Finisher's Habit: damage bonus against wounded elites
- Front Sight Faith: bonus accuracy after maintaining Clean or better condition

## Frontiersman Additions
- Long Barrel Walker: reduced accuracy loss after long horseback travel
- Saddle Draw: faster draw speed after dismounting
- Camp Mechanic: lower workbench tuning cost
- Trail Toolman: higher salvage yield from abandoned wagons and camps

## Survivor Additions
- Iron Shoulder: reduced stagger from shotgun and rifle hits
- Bitter Recovery: temporary resist after surviving critical health
- Patch Job: field repairs restore more weapon condition
- Last Clean Gun: small combat bonus when all equipped weapons are maintained

## Outlaw Additions
- Fast Hands: quicker loot and weapon strip time after kills
- Hard Exit: short damage bonus after point-blank kill or carriage robbery
- Loose Morals: more value from stolen weapon parts
- Dirty Trigger: chance for extra fear effect when attacking weaker enemies

## Relic Hunter Additions
- Mark Reader: named enemies are easier to identify on first sight
- Cold Etching: relic-bound weapons gain higher bonus ceilings
- Grave Metal: boss-crafted weapons lose condition more slowly
- Storm Wake: late-game mythic kill bonus against special boss-class enemies

Can branch into:
- lawman precision

### Favorite Sidearm Path
A single revolver can become:

- signature grips

## Why This Fits Red Dead
This works because the fantasy is not "future gunsmith sandbox."
The fantasy is:

"John Marston keeps his guns alive, rebuilds them from frontier scrap, and turns them into the tools he needs to survive a harsher West."

That is very Red Dead, just deeper.

## Final Design Summary
Yes, Fallout-like gun management, modding, crafting, and expanded perk integration can fit this overhaul.
The correct version is a frontier armory system built around Beecher's Hope, gunsmith specialists, salvaged parts, blueprint hunting, and named weapon progression.
