# RDR2 Epilogue RPG Overhaul First 10 Hours Draft

## Goal
The first 10 hours must do four things well:

- make the player believe the world has changed
- turn Beecher's Hope into a meaningful home hub
- connect the new RPG loop to the existing epilogue sandbox
- make every installed activity mod feel like part of one larger frontier system

This draft assumes John Marston in the epilogue as the only playable protagonist.

## The Main Gameplay Loop

The player loop is:

1. hear rumors or take ledger work
2. travel to a region
3. fight or investigate
4. loot materials, relics, and intel
5. return to Beecher's Hope or a support town
6. upgrade John, gear, and the ledger
7. unlock harder work, elites, and boss clues

This repeats across the existing map without needing new land.

## Hour 0 To 1: Ashes At Beecher's Hope

### Opening Incident
One night, Beecher's Hope is hit by a small but unusually organized raid.
The attackers are not normal bandits. They search drawers, ledgers, old lockboxes, and Jack's room instead of just stealing cash and food.

### Immediate Objectives
- defend the ranch
- inspect the bodies
- find the marked ledger
- speak to Abigail, Uncle, and Jack

### Family Roles In The Opening
- Abigail pushes John to stop whatever is coming before it reaches the house again
- Jack becomes obsessed with the strange symbols and sketches them into the ledger
- Uncle claims he has heard drunk men in Blackwater talking about "relic money" and "sky devils"

### Systems Unlocked
- Beecher's Hope hub
- Frontier Ledger
- basic threat meter
- first accessory slot
- first region clue
- first visible family affinity bars

### Intended Player Feeling
The player should feel that the epilogue ranch is no longer just a home.
It is now the center of a frontier war John did not ask for.

## Hour 1 To 2: First Steps Into The New Loop

### Core Tutorial Tasks
The player is sent on three grounded jobs:
- recover a dead courier's satchel near Blackwater
- clear a small scavenger camp in Great Plains
- bring back upgrade materials for the Beecher's Hope workbench

### What This Teaches
- combat drops matter
- materials now matter
- clues point toward larger targets
- Beecher's Hope upgrades are permanent progression
- gifts and dialogue now affect family trust

### First Repeatable Content Hooks
- bounty-style work begins feeding badges and target intel
- stagecoach work begins feeding locked crates and trade goods
- jobs begin feeding wages, tool parts, and supply tokens

This is where the installed activity mods begin feeling like one system instead of separate features.

## Hour 2 To 4: Blackwater Pressure

### Story Movement
Blackwater becomes the first real city node.
John learns that multiple groups are chasing the same caches.
A local broker is paying extra for sealed lockboxes, saint medals, military watch parts, and marked gang rings.

### New Activities
- first elite bounty target
- first duel challenge tied to reputation
- first contract assassination lead
- first heavy stagecoach cargo robbery

### First Region Identity
West Elizabeth becomes the beginner frontier zone.
It is safer than New Austin but clearly getting uglier.

### First Named Elite
The player meets the first mini-boss tier enemy:
Rusk "The Collector's Dog"

He is not the mastermind.
He is the first sign that named enemies carry:
- better health
- stronger gear
- unique drops
- their own identity

### Reward Tier
By the end of this phase, the player should have:
- one upgraded weapon path
- one or two accessories
- enough materials to understand the crafting economy
- their first real reason to revisit Beecher's Hope

## Hour 4 To 6: The Frontier Opens

### New Regions Start Mattering
The player begins branching between:
- Great Plains and Tall Trees for contracts and ambush routes
- New Austin for risky salvage and outlaw loot
- New Hanover edges for hunting, ore, and veteran gunmen

### New Systems Unlocked
- perk points
- accessory management
- elite enemy titles
- regional threat levels
- first boss clue chain

### Activity Integration
Installed mod content becomes categorized in the ledger as:
- Frontier Work: jobs and legal contracts
- Blood Work: bounties, duels, targeted killings
- Supply Work: stagecoaches, wagon raids, stolen cargo
- Hidden Work: relic recovery, clue hunts, boss unlock chains

This creates a coherent identity for the existing installed mods.

## Hour 6 To 8: The Ranch Becomes A War Room

### Beecher's Hope Progression
Returning home now changes the ranch visually and mechanically.

Possible upgrades in this phase:
- map board
- better workbench
- hidden stash chest
- trophy wall
- stronger perimeter lighting
- medicine cabinet

### Family Integration
- Abigail unlocks Homefront tasks that improve healing, supplies, and house safety
- Jack begins adding sketches to the Ledger of the Dead, revealing boss silhouettes and rumors
- Uncle unlocks dirty routes, low-trust buyers, and rumor-based side leads
- their affinity stages now start affecting repeatable payout quality and future dedicated reward quests

### Midgame Content Pressure
The player is now strong enough to start:
- taking elite hunts
- entering relic zones at night
- tackling dangerous convoy fights
- fighting enforcers and veteran squads

## Hour 8 To 10: First Boss Arc

### Boss Arc Structure
The first major boss should require more than just a marker.

Example chain:
- recover three clues from separate activities
- identify which region the boss controls
- weaken the boss by intercepting supplies
- enter the boss territory under special conditions
- fight the boss with elite support enemies

### First Boss Concept
Silas Redd, The Fence of Ash

Role:
- ex-military looter
- runs relic traffic through Great Plains and Blackwater
- employs contract gunmen and scorched-earth raiders

Fight Identity:
- grounded human boss
- durable, accurate, aggressive
- uses reinforced gear and high-end firearms
- supported by named lieutenants

### First Major Payoff
Defeating Silas Redd unlocks:
- second accessory row
- advanced workbench recipes
- faction reputation visibility
- new late-game rumor chain
- the first true hint that the "sky devil" rumors may not be nonsense

## First 10 Hours Summary By Feel

### Hours 0 To 2
Home invasion, shock, tutorial, first materials, first clue

### Hours 2 To 4
Blackwater opens, bounty and contract loop starts, first named elite

### Hours 4 To 6
Whole frontier loop opens, perks and progression matter

### Hours 6 To 8
Beecher's Hope becomes home base and war room

### Hours 8 To 10
First boss arc completes and the true larger conflict is revealed

## How The Installed Mods Fit The Loop

### Bounties Expansion
Feeds elite targets, badges, wanted intel, and faction names

### Jobs
Feeds steady cash, tools, household supplies, and crafting parts

### Contracts Remastered
Feeds named kills, stealth rewards, clue fragments, and black-market routes

### Stagecoach Robberies
Feeds cargo crates, rare trade goods, and ambush sequences

### Ambient Money Stagecoaches
Feeds chance-based jackpot runs and surprise resource windfalls

### Duels
Feeds reputation, gunslinger marks, and special trinkets

### Companion System
Can be repurposed for temporary hired support on high-tier runs

### Buyable Properties
Works as optional regional stash houses and safe drop points

### SgtJoe Bank Robberies
Acts as high-risk, high-value late phase supply raids

## What Must Be True After 10 Hours

- the player understands why looting matters
- Beecher's Hope feels alive and useful
- family members matter without becoming action-game followers
- installed content feels unified
- elites feel different from normal enemies
- the first boss proves the mod has a real long-term arc
- the player wants to chase the first mythic rumor next
