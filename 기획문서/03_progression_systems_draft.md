# RDR2 Epilogue RPG Overhaul Progression Systems Draft

## Design Goal
The progression systems must feel deeper than vanilla without feeling like a disconnected fantasy MMO.
Everything should read as frontier survival, outlaw reputation, gunsmithing, fieldcraft, and dangerous relic obsession.

## Character Level Structure

### Leveling Model
Use a single overall frontier level plus individual stat growth.

The player earns:
- frontier experience
- perk points
- material knowledge
- faction standing

### Experience Sources
- bounties
- contracts
- elite kills
- boss kills
- relic discoveries
- rare material turn-ins
- region event clears
- house and workshop progression milestones

## Core Stats

## 1. Strength
Meaning:
- melee damage
- tackle power
- carry bonus
- forced-open interactions

How it grows:
- melee kills
- heavy labor jobs
- hauling salvage
- close-range combat challenges

## 2. Grit
Meaning:
- max health
- wound resistance
- recovery speed
- resistance to stagger and panic

How it grows:
- surviving difficult fights
- taking damage and winning
- defending Beecher's Hope
- finishing high-threat contracts

## 3. Deadeye
Meaning:
- aiming stability
- weak-point damage
- reload speed
- critical chance on marked enemies

How it grows:
- precision kills
- duels
- headshot streaks
- elite target kills

## 4. Survival
Meaning:
- tracking strength
- pelt and harvest yield
- environmental resistance
- special resource detection

How it grows:
- hunting
- gathering
- campcraft tasks
- wilderness objectives

## 5. Cunning
Meaning:
- price bonuses
- clue reading
- hidden stash detection
- ambush warning
- negotiation and intel rewards

How it grows:
- investigation
- selling rare items
- completing contracts cleanly
- deciphering rumors and notes

## Perk Tree Structure

### Tree 1: Gunslinger
Identity:
precision and aggression

Example perks:
- Quick Draw: faster ready time after a holster draw
- Iron Wrist: reduced recoil on revolvers and pistols
- Eye For Weakness: bonus damage to elites after a tagged crit
- Last Word: bonus damage on the final round in a weapon

### Tree 2: Frontiersman
Identity:
field utility and mobility

Example perks:
- Long Trail: reduced stamina drain while traveling
- Sharp Tracker: better clue visibility and longer enemy trail persistence
- Hard Saddle: less horse panic under pressure
- Dust Reader: reveals rare event zones on the frontier map

### Tree 3: Survivor
Identity:
toughness and sustain

Example perks:
- Thick Skin: flat damage reduction against common enemies
- Hold The Line: resistance to stagger while aiming
- Dry Blood: improved healing item efficiency
- Ranch Resolve: combat bonuses while defending allied property

### Tree 4: Outlaw
Identity:
risk, robbery, and intimidation

Example perks:
- Cold Voice: chance to scare weak enemies during standoffs
- Clean Hands, Dirty Money: higher loot from supply raids
- Fast Pockets: quicker looting under pressure
- One More Lockbox: extra reward roll on cargo targets

### Tree 5: Relic Hunter
Identity:
late-game mythic progression

Example perks:
- Strange Pull: greater chance to detect hidden relic caches
- Bone Reader: accessory drops have a higher quality floor
- Grave Luck: better boss material yields
- Ash Mark: bonus damage against named enemies and boss adds

## Gear Structure

## Equipment Philosophy
Do not add cartoon armor classes.
Keep gear grounded in western language.

Use:
- coats
- vests
- holsters
- gloves
- charms
- tokens
- talismans
- watch pieces
- satchel tools

## Gear Slots
- Major Accessory 1
- Major Accessory 2
- Minor Charm 1
- Minor Charm 2
- Holster Mod
- Satchel Mod
- Coat Lining
- Saddle Token

## Gear Rarity Style
Use grounded rarity names:
- Worn
- Reliable
- Fine
- Masterwork
- Relic
- Legendary

## Example Accessory Types
- Saint Medal: small Grit bonus, anti-fear utility
- Gambler Token: duel and crit bonus
- Bone Charm: hunting and Survival bonus
- Officer's Pocketwatch: Cunning and clue bonus
- Reinforced Gun Belt Plate: reload and holster speed bonus
- Trapper Sigil: pelt quality and carry efficiency bonus

## Upgrade Structure

## Upgrade Sources
Upgrades are installed through:
- Beecher's Hope workbench
- gunsmith
- trapper
- fence
- special boss recipes

## Upgrade Categories

### Weapon Upgrades
- rifling kits
- reinforced frames
- rare grips
- balance tuning
- sight tuning
- relic-infused variants for late game

### Gear Upgrades
- coat lining reinforcement
- satchel expansion
- holster rig tuning
- saddle reinforcement
- trinket socket unlocks

### Home Upgrades
- workbench tier
- medical cabinet
- stash capacity
- rumor board
- trophy board

## Enhancement Language
Instead of calling everything enchantment, use terms that fit the setting:
- tempering
- binding
- etching
- sealing
- fitting
- blessing

That lets some items stay grounded while a few late-game relics edge into myth.

## Rare Materials

## Farming Sources
- hunting
- gang hideouts
- stagecoach cargo
- bank and wagon loot
- elite enemies
- contracts
- town black markets
- caves and mines
- boss drops

## Farming Should Feel Good
The player should always know why they are farming.
Every run should feed one or more of these:
- a better weapon
- a stronger accessory
- a Beecher's Hope upgrade
- a perk unlock
- a boss summon or clue

## Enemy Structure

## Common Enemies
Basic drifters and raiders.
They provide light materials and low-value loot.

## Veterans
More disciplined and better armed.
They provide better parts and better ammo.

## Enforcers
Heavy melee or shotgun threats with strong health pools.
They provide gear components and reinforced materials.

## Named Elites
Mini-boss enemies with custom titles, better AI pressure, and unique drops.

## Bosses
Story-defining encounters with:
- mechanics
- phases
- unique sound and mood
- signature drops
- frontier legend entries

## Combat Tuning Goal
Enemies should not just become damage sponges.
They must become:
- more accurate
- more aggressive
- better at flanking
- more dangerous in groups
- more distinct by role

## System Identity In One Line
The player grows stronger not by abstract loot spam, but by surviving the new frontier economy of violence, scavenging, and legend-making.
